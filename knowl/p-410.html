In the complex vector space \(\CV{n}\) this extends naturally, and for each \(\vv\in\CV{n}\) we define the conjugate of \(\vv\) to be the unique vector \(\conj{\vv}\in\CV{n}\) satisfying
\begin{equation*} \entry{\conj{\vv}}{k} = \conj{\entry{\vv}{k}} \end{equation*}
for every \({k\in\set{0,1,2,\dotsc,n-1}}\text{.}\)
