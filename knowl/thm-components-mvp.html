Theorem 4.3.6. Components of a matrix-vector product.

Let \(\vec{A}_0,\vec{A}_1,\dotsc,\vec{A}_{n-1}\in\CV{m}\) be vectors such that
\begin{equation*} A=\left[\vec{A}_1|\cdots|\vec{A}_{n-1}\right]\in\Mats{m,n} \end{equation*}
and let \(\vv\in\CV{n}\text{.}\) Then for every \({r\in\set{0,\dotsc,m-1}}\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \entry{A\vv}{r} = \entry{\vv}{0}\entry{A}{r,0}+\entry{\vv}{1}\entry{A}{r,1} + \cdots + \entry{\vv}{n-1}\entry{A}{r,n-1} = \sum_{c=0}^{n-1} \entry{A}{r,c}\entry{\vv}{c}\text{.} \end{equation*}
