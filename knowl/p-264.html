As long as their has been a need to communicate information, there has been a desire to hide that communication from unintended recipients. Cryptography, loosely translated from the Greek roots as secret writing, is the study and implementation of secure communication in the possible presence of adversaries. We will start by extending our investigation of permutations into their application to cryptography before moving through more complicated permutation-based cryptography.
