Algorithm 1.2.12. Euclid's Algorithm.

Let \(a,b\in\Zp\) be given. By repeatedly applying The Division Algorithm to the pair \(a,b\text{,}\) produce the sequences \((q_1,q_2,\dotsc,q_k)\) and \((r_1,r_2,\dotsc,r_k)\) where \(r_k=0\text{,}\) such that
\begin{align*} a \amp= b \cdot q_1 + r_1\\ b \amp= r_1 \cdot q_2 + r_2\\ r_1 \amp= r_2 \cdot q_3 + r_3\\ \end{align*}

and so on until

\begin{align*} r_{k-3} \amp= r_{k-2} \cdot q_{k-1} + r_{k-1}\\ r_{k-2} \amp= r_{k-1} \cdot q_k + r_k \\ &= r_{k-1}\cdot q_k\text{.} \end{align*}
Then \(\gcd(a,b) = r_{k-1}\text{.}\)

Proof.
