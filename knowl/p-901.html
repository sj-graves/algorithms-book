In mathematics prior to Calculus, the basic type of things you study are numbers, pretty vaguely defined, and then in calculus you're introduced to the idea of sets of numbers:
\begin{align*} \amp\Nats = \set{0,1,2,3,\dotsc},\\ \amp\Ints = \set{\dotsc,-2,-1,0,1,2,\dotsc},\\ \amp\Rats = \set{p/q:p,q\in\Ints,q\neq 0},\\ \amp\Reals,{}\text{ the set of all real numbers},\\ \amp\Comps = \set{a+ib:a,b\in\Reals, i^2=-1}. \end{align*}
