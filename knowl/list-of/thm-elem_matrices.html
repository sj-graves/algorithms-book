Theorem 5.1.7. Elementary matrices.

Let \(I_n\in\Mats{m,m}\) be the unique identity matrix of size \(m\text{,}\) satisfying
\begin{equation*} \entry{I_m}{r,c} = \begin{cases}1,\amp r=c\\ 0,\amp r\neq c\end{cases}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Moreover, let
\begin{align*} I_m \amp\xto{\rswap{k}{\ell}} S_{k,\ell} \amp I_m \amp\xto{\rscale{\alpha}{k}} M_{\alpha, k} \amp I_m \amp\xto{\relim{\alpha}{k}{\ell}} E_{\alpha,k,\ell}\text{.} \end{align*}
Then for any \(A\in\Mats{m,n}\text{,}\)
\begin{align*} A\amp\xto{\rswap{k}{\ell}} S_{k,\ell} A \amp A\amp\xto{\rscale{\alpha}{k}} M_{\alpha,k} A \amp A\amp\xto{\relim{\alpha}{k}{\ell}} E_{\alpha,k,\ell} A\text{.} \end{align*}
